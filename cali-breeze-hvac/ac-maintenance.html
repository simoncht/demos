Keep your cooling system running at its best

We give you a straight read on your system — not a list of upsells. If it's running fine, we'll tell you that too.

When to schedule a tune-up

Ideally twice a year — once in spring before cooling season begins, and again in fall before you switch to heat. At minimum, schedule an AC tune-up each spring so you're not discovering problems on the first hot day of the year. North County summers can be brutal; don't find out your system has a weak capacitor at 4 PM on a 95-degree Saturday.

What our AC tune-up includes

  • Evaporator and condenser coil cleaning — dirty coils force your system to work harder and drive up your bill
  • Refrigerant level check — low refrigerant means poor cooling and compressor wear; we verify the charge is correct
  • Electrical and capacitor inspection — capacitors are the most common single-point failure in AC units; we test them before they fail on you
  • Filter inspection — a clogged filter kills airflow and efficiency; we check it and advise on replacement interval
  • Thermostat calibration check — making sure the unit cycles on and off at the right temperatures
  • Blower motor and belt inspection
  • Condensate drain line check and flush
  • Overall system performance test — temperature differential across supply and return air

Why regular maintenance pays off

Lower energy bills. A clean, properly charged system uses less electricity to produce the same cooling. Dirty coils and low refrigerant are silent bill-raisers.

Fewer breakdowns. The tune-up catches the worn parts — capacitors, contactors, belts — before they fail. Catching a $40 capacitor in April is a lot cheaper than an emergency call in July.

Longer system life. Systems that are maintained regularly routinely outlast ones that aren't. That extends the time before you're looking at a $5,000–$10,000 replacement.

Warranty protection. Many manufacturers require documented annual maintenance to keep the equipment warranty valid. We can tell you what your system requires.

Frequently asked questions

How often should I schedule AC maintenance?
At minimum once a year, ideally in spring before cooling season. Twice a year — spring for AC, fall for heating — is ideal for systems that run year-round, which is most of North County. Cali Breeze serves Fallbrook, Vista, Oceanside, Carlsbad, Escondido, Encinitas, San Marcos, and Temecula.
What is included in a tune-up?
We do a full inspection: coil cleaning (evaporator and condenser), refrigerant level check, electrical and capacitor testing, filter inspection, thermostat calibration, blower motor check, condensate drain flush, and an overall system performance test. We tell you what we find — no fluff, no upsells.
Why is regular AC maintenance important?
Routine maintenance boosts energy efficiency, cuts the chance of a breakdown, and extends your system's life. It also helps catch small issues — like a weak capacitor or a dirty coil — before they turn into expensive repairs or a premature replacement.
Can maintenance prevent costly repairs?
Yes. Regular tune-ups let us identify minor issues early. Replacing a worn capacitor during a scheduled visit is far cheaper than an emergency call when it fails on the hottest day of the year. Most homeowners find the tune-up pays for itself quickly.
