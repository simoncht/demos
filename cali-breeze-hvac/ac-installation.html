Should You Repair or Replace?

This is the question we get asked most. Here's how we look at it:

  • Systems 10–15 years old often make more financial sense to replace than keep repairing
  • If repair costs are approaching half the price of a new system, replacement typically wins long-term
  • A newer high-efficiency system (higher SEER rating) can meaningfully reduce your monthly energy bills — especially running through Fallbrook's hot summers
  • If your current system still has good life left, we'll tell you that too — we don't pressure replace

We'll give you our honest read on what makes sense for your situation, your budget, and your home.

Common Questions

How do I know if I need a new AC installation or just a repair?
If your system is over 10–15 years old, needs frequent repairs, or struggles to cool your home effectively, replacement is often the better investment. Cali Breeze will evaluate your existing system and give you an honest assessment — we don't push replacement if repair makes more sense.
How long does an AC installation or replacement take?
Most residential installations are completed in a single day. The timeline can vary depending on the system type and your home, but we work efficiently and don't leave jobs half-finished.
What type of AC system is right for my home?
It depends on your home's size, layout, existing ductwork, and budget. We install central air conditioning systems, ductless mini-splits, and other modern cooling options. We'll recommend what actually fits your situation — not the most expensive option by default.
Is AC replacement better than repair?
If repair costs are high relative to the system's remaining useful life — or if the unit is 10–15+ years old — replacement usually wins over time through lower energy bills and reduced breakdown risk. We give you both numbers honestly so you can decide.
Do you offer financing on new AC systems?
Yes. Financing options are available to help spread the cost of a new system. Ask us when you call or visit our financing page for details.
