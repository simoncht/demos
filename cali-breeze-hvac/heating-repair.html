Is Your Heater Struggling? Here's What to Watch For

San Diego nights can get cold fast, especially in Fallbrook and the inland hills. When your heating system isn't keeping up, the signs are usually clear:

  • No heat at all, or only weak, lukewarm airflow
  • System keeps cycling on and off (short cycling)
  • Unusual noises — banging, rattling, or squealing
  • Strange odors, especially a sulfur or burning smell
  • Uneven heating — some rooms warm, others cold
  • Energy bills climbing with no change in usage
  • Yellow or orange burner flame instead of blue
A yellow burner flame is a potential carbon-monoxide warning sign. If you see one, turn the system off and call us — we'll come out and check it safely.

Gas & Electric Furnaces

Furnace problems usually come down to a handful of root causes: a faulty ignitor, a cracked heat exchanger, a dirty flame sensor, or a failing blower motor. We diagnose the actual problem — not just the symptom — and tell you straight what it will cost to fix it before we touch anything.

Heat Pumps

Heat pumps move warmth rather than generate it, so diagnosing them takes a different skill set. Our techs are trained on both the refrigerant side and the electrical side, covering reversing valves, defrost controls, and auxiliary heat failures.

Safety Checks on Every Visit

On every heating repair call we check for gas leaks around connections and valves, and we test for carbon monoxide at the heat exchanger. If we find a crack or a hazard, we tell you plainly — and we won't use it to upsell you a system you don't need.

Repair vs. Replace — the Honest Answer

If your furnace is over 10–15 years old and the repair cost is climbing toward 50% of what a new unit would run, replacement usually makes more financial sense. We'll do the math with you on the spot. If a repair is the right call, we say so. If it isn't, we say that too.

FAQ

Heating repair questions, answered honestly

Why is my heater not blowing hot air?
The most common causes are a faulty thermostat, a clogged filter blocking airflow, a pilot light or ignitor failure, or a malfunctioning furnace component. A tech can usually pinpoint it on the first visit and tell you whether it's a simple fix or something more serious.
What does a yellow burner flame mean — is it dangerous?
A healthy gas burner flame is blue. A yellow or orange flame can indicate incomplete combustion, which may produce carbon monoxide. Turn the system off and call us. We carry CO detection equipment and will check it safely before determining next steps.
Do I need heater repair or a full replacement?
A good rule of thumb: if the repair cost is approaching 50% of what a new unit would cost — and your system is over 10–15 years old — replacement usually wins financially. We'll give you both numbers clearly so you can decide. No pressure in either direction.
How quickly can I get emergency heating repair?
We offer same-day service when you contact us before 2 PM. Call (760) 643-8184 and we'll do our best to get a tech to you the same day, Monday through Saturday.
What are common signs my furnace needs repair?
Strange noises (banging, rattling, squealing), uneven heating between rooms, short cycling (turns on and off frequently), rising energy bills without a usage change, and no heat at all are the main ones. The sooner you catch them, the less expensive the fix tends to be.
Do you service heat pumps as well as furnaces?
Yes. Our techs are trained on both furnaces (gas and electric) and heat pumps, including reversing valve failures, defrost control issues, and auxiliary/emergency heat problems. We also service the major brands: Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, Goodman, Bryant, Day & Night, and Mitsubishi Electric.
