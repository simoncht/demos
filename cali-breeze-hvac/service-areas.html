Common questions

HVAC Questions, Answered Straight

No upselling, no scare tactics — just honest answers from a team that's been doing this for 15+ years.

What is the $5,000 rule for AC?

One common guideline we use is the "$5,000 Rule." It suggests that if repairing your current AC system costs more than $5,000, it's usually more cost-effective to invest in a new, energy-efficient model. But it's not an absolute rule. Our certified technicians consider your system's age, condition, repair history, and energy efficiency to recommend the best solution — not just the most expensive one.

What's the most expensive AC part to repair?

The compressor is typically the most expensive part to replace — it can cost up to $4,000. It's responsible for moving refrigerant between the evaporator and condenser coils, so it's essential to how your system cools. The best way to extend your compressor's lifespan is regular maintenance. We help homeowners across Fallbrook, Vista, and Oceanside stay ahead of expensive repairs with expert AC tune-ups.

How much does it cost to fix an AC in California?

Costs vary based on the issue and system type. Typically, homeowners spend between $150 and $1,200 on repairs. Small fixes — like replacing capacitors, cleaning drain lines, or fixing thermostats — usually run under $500. Major part replacements such as compressors or evaporator coils can run $2,000 or more. Routine maintenance is the best way to prevent costly breakdowns and extend your system's lifespan.

How do I know if my HVAC system needs repair or replacement?

Signs that your system might need repair include strange noises, frequent cycling, higher energy bills, and decreased airflow or comfort. If repairs are happening often or your system is over 15 years old, replacement could be more cost-effective in the long run. Newer systems are more energy-efficient and can save you money on utility bills. A Cali Breeze technician can assess your system and give you a straight answer on whether to repair or replace.

Can upgrading my HVAC system reduce my energy bills?

Yes. Modern HVAC units are built to use less energy while providing better climate control than older models. Features like programmable thermostats, higher SEER ratings, and improved system designs all help reduce energy use over time. We can evaluate your current system and walk you through energy-efficient options that make sense for your home and budget — no pressure.

How long does an HVAC installation take?

Replacing an old HVAC system with a new one in a standard residential home typically takes between 4 and 8 hours. More complex installations — such as those involving extensive ductwork changes or zoning systems — may take a day or two. After assessing your specific setup, our team will give you a realistic time estimate upfront.
