What We Check During Every Maintenance Visit

Our thorough inspection covers every part of your heating system. Here's what a Cali Breeze tune-up includes:

  • Full safety inspection — gas connections, heat exchanger, CO levels
  • Burner cleaning and combustion analysis
  • Air filter inspection and replacement (if needed)
  • Blower motor and belt check
  • Airflow measurement and duct inspection
  • Thermostat calibration and test
  • Igniter and flame sensor inspection
  • Flue and venting check for blockages or leaks
  • Electrical connections — tighten and test all terminals
  • Overall efficiency assessment with written findings

Why Schedule Before Winter?

Fall is the ideal time for a heating tune-up in North San Diego County. By the time the temperature drops in Fallbrook and the inland valleys, HVAC schedules fill fast. A pre-season visit means:

  • Priority scheduling — you pick the day, no waiting in a queue
  • Safety peace of mind — gas and CO issues found before the season starts
  • Lower energy bills — a clean, calibrated system runs more efficiently
  • Longer system life — small wear caught early avoids big failures later
  • Warranty compliance — many manufacturers require annual maintenance

How Often Should You Service Your Heater?

Once a year, ideally in fall before you need it. That's the standard recommended by every major manufacturer — Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, Goodman, Bryant, Day & Night, and Mitsubishi Electric — and it's what keeps most manufacturer warranties valid. If your system is older or running harder than usual, we may suggest a mid-season check as well. We'll give you an honest read, not a calendar designed to sell you appointments.

Extending Your System's Life

Most heating systems last 15–20 years with regular care. Skipping maintenance shrinks that window significantly. Cali Breeze techs Davie and Ricky have seen it both ways — systems that outlast their expected life because an owner kept up with tune-ups, and systems that failed early from nothing but neglect. A $100–$150 annual visit is consistently the cheapest way to protect a $3,000–$8,000 piece of equipment.

Annual maintenance is the single best investment you can make in a heating system you already own. We'll tell you if yours needs more — and equally important, we'll tell you when it doesn't.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I schedule heater maintenance?
Once a year is the standard recommendation — ideally in fall, before you turn it on for the season. Annual service keeps your system efficient, keeps warranties valid, and lets us catch small issues before they become expensive repairs.
What does a heater maintenance visit include?
We inspect the full system: clean the burners, check the heat exchanger and gas connections, test safety controls, measure airflow, calibrate the thermostat, inspect the igniter and flame sensor, and check the flue for blockages. You get a written summary of what we found.
Why is regular maintenance important?
Regular tune-ups boost efficiency, prevent unexpected breakdowns, prolong equipment life, and lower energy bills. They also keep most manufacturer warranties from being voided by lack of documented service.
Can maintenance prevent costly repairs?
Yes — identifying small issues early (a worn igniter, a loose connection, a dirty flame sensor) is consistently cheaper than the breakdown those issues cause. It also means you're not without heat on the coldest night of the year.
