Good to know

Frequently asked questions

Do you take my vision insurance?
We accept VSP, EyeMed, and most major vision and medical plans. Tell us your provider when you book and we'll verify your benefits before your visit, so there are no surprises.
How often should I get an eye exam?
Most adults benefit from a comprehensive exam every 1–2 years, and annually if you wear contacts, have diabetes, or are over 60. Kids should be checked before starting school and regularly after.
Can I get my exam and new glasses in one visit?
Usually, yes. After your exam you can browse frames and our opticians will help you choose and order the same day. Many lens options are available quickly.
Do I need an appointment, or do you take walk-ins?
We recommend booking so the doctor can give you unhurried time, but we do our best to help walk-ins and urgent red-eye visits — just call ahead.
What should I bring to my exam?
Bring your insurance card (if using), your current glasses or contact boxes, and a list of any medications. Plan for about 30–45 minutes.
