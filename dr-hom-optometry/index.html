The doctor

Gregory G. Hom, OD, MPH, FAAO

"To provide our patients good value by delivering professional care and quality products, with a focus on promoting eye health and safety."

A third-generation San Diegan, Dr. Hom studied biology at UC San Diego, earned his Doctor of Optometry from UC Berkeley (1991) and a Master of Public Health from SDSU (1993). He is California Board–certified in the treatment and management of eye disease and a Fellow of the American Academy of Optometry.

He has served as Past-President of the San Diego County Optometric Society and Past-Chair of the Vision Care Section of the American Public Health Association. His public-health background — including work in laboratory safety and office ergonomics — shapes a careful, prevention-minded approach to your vision. Every visit is unhurried, so you get real time with the doctor.

Frequently asked questions

Do you take my vision insurance?
The office accepts VSP, EyeMed, Medicare, and most major vision and medical plans. Share your provider when you book and the team will verify your benefits before your visit.
How far out are you booking?
The schedule is currently booking from September — a reflection of how many patients trust Dr. Hom with their care. Visits are by appointment so each patient gets unhurried time. To be seen sooner, join the cancellation list and we'll notify you the moment an earlier slot opens. For an urgent eye issue, call the office right away.
How often should I have an eye exam?
Most adults benefit from a comprehensive exam every 1–2 years, and annually if you wear contacts, have diabetes, or are over 60. Regular exams help catch issues early.
Can Dr. Hom advise me on LASIK?
Yes. Dr. Hom offers honest, independent guidance on whether laser vision correction is a good option for you and what to expect — with no pressure.
What should I bring to my visit?
Bring your insurance card (if using), your current glasses or contact boxes, and a list of any medications. Plan for about 30–45 minutes.
